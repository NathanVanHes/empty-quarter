SLIDE 04 / 12 · The Pythagorean identity

The unit circle is a compass for trigonometry.

Every point on the unit circle gives us a sine, a cosine, and a relationship. If we know any two, the third follows. This is the first identity — the Pythagorean one — and every other identity grows from it.

Identity I · Pythagorean
sin2θ + cos2θ = 1
// for all θ ∈ ℝ, derived from x² + y² = r² on a unit circle
